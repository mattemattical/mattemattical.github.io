Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

The following are the frequently asked question and its corresponding answers

Global Warming and Carbon Emissions
What is Carbon Emission?

Carbon emission is the release of carbon into the atmosphere. It contributes to the warming of the earth surfaces. It surface has been observed since the pre-industrial period which causes climate change, rising sea levels, and melting of ice. This is due to the increased carbon emission that started since the industrial revolution where burning fossil fuels have increased.

How carbon emission contributes global warming?

ACarbon dioxide (CO2) becomes a major contributor to air pollution because of its role in the greenhouse effect in which it traps the radiation coming from the sun into a ground-level creating a ground-level ozone. [2] This condition in the atmospheric layer of the Earth prevents the planet from cooling at night resulting to global warming which affects the climate of the Earth thus developing climate change.

How urgent is global warming?

Global warming has been a major problem of the world in our present age. Greenhouse gases, especially carbon dioxide (CO2) are the major contributor to global warming. Greenhouse gases are gases that are responsible for the greenhouse effect, in which these gases trap heat in the earth's atmosphere to make the earth stay warm, but an excessive amount of these gases may result in unnatural warming of the earth and irregulates on the climate cycle of the planet thus resulting into global warming and climate change

Carbon Capture and the Device
What is carbon capture?

Carbon Capture is the process of capturing carbon dioxide and feeding it into the algae culture for carbon absorption .

What does the Smart Algae Cultivation System really do?

It will cultivate a strain of algae in order to control the parameters that affects its growth thus preventing it in leading into algal bloom.

What is the byproduct of the Device?

The algal biomass harvested can be a great opportunity for success for entrepreneurs and business owners, even aspiring ones, because the algal biomass harvested can be used for several production. These products depend on the kind of algae strain being used.

About Algae
What are Algae?

Algae are a diverse group of aquatic organisms that have the ability to conduct photosynthesis. Certain algae are familiar to most people; for instance, seaweeds (such as kelp or phytoplankton), pond scum or the algal blooms in lakes. However, there exists a vast and varied world of algae that are not only helpful to us, but are critical to our existence.

Why algae is efficient solution in global warming?

Algae is an efficient solution as a replacement for trees due its ability to absorb carbon dioxide 400 times much better than the trees because of the speed of its growth rate and the larger surface area it can cover in the environment because it grows on water.

What is Algal Bloom?

Algal Bloom is rapid growth of algae in terms of its population in aquatic ecosystems mainly composed of biotoxins that can be distinguished by the discoloration of water. The device has a smart monitoring system that prevents algal bloom.

About the Study
What is the study all about?

The research aims to develop a Smart Algae Cultivation System and CO2 Capture Technology for Quarantine Facility. The Following are the specific objectives:
1. Create a smart algae cultivation device comprising of Flat-plate Photobioreactor (PBR), LED panels, solar panels, sensors, and microcontroller.
2. Develop an IoT-based mobile application for monitoring, data visualization, and notification to the end-user using ThingSpeak and MIT app inventor.
3. Compare the concentration of carbon dioxide (CO2) present in the atmosphere in an enclosed vicinity before and after deployment of the device.
4. Test the accuracy and efficiency of the device in terms of CO2 capture and algae cultivation
5. Evaluate the mobile application in accordance with the ISO standard 25010.

What strain of algae used?

The strain of algae that is used in this study is Chlorella ellipsoidea, other strains of algae and its potential are part of the limitation of this study.
